How to Help

At the heart of biodiversity, ecosystem health, and our own food production is the honey bee, along side other pollinating insects, birds and bats. Together, we can all do our part to help save honey bees and other pollinators, after all, we all share this home called Earth. By helping each other to thrive, we can live in harmony for generations to come. Here are a few easy ways that you can help:

  1. Support local beekeepers who take care of honey bees by buying their products
  2. Plant flowers that provide food for pollinators. Some of my favourites include:
    • Purple Cone Flower
    • New England Aster
    • Blazing Star
  3. Avoid using pesticides and other chemicals on your lawn and garden
  4. Avoid spraying or weeding Dandelions, they are a great early food source for pollinators!
  5. Create habitat for pollinators. Build a wild bee house, leave your garden clippings at the end of the season, leave un-mulched areas for ground nesting insects, and leave areas of your property to grow wild with native grasses and flowers
  6. Avoid buying seeds and plants treated with neonicotinoids. Ask someone at your local greenhouse if you are not sure.
  7. Ask the government to ban honey bee killing pesticides
  8. Participate in Citizen Science and report your pollinator sightings online so researchers can better detect the range of various species
  9. Educate others!

Thanks for your help saving the bees!
